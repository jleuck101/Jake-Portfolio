Portfolio Summary:
Rebuilt and extended a moving aerial-style shot using a combination of camera tracking, projected environment elements, mirrored/extended building work, sky replacement, and multiple foreground holdouts. Solved the plate in 3D to support projected fixes and extensions, preserved and layered foreground powerlines back over the new sky, and balanced the final image with volumetric light, flare, focus, vignette, and regrain/lensing treatment. The shot is strongest as an example of combining matchmove, projection, and compositing cleanup to stabilize a more complex moving environment shot.

What I Did:
- Camera tracked / matchmoved the moving plate
- Rebuilt and extended background / building areas using projected elements
- Used mirrored/tracked building reconstruction to expand or repair architecture
- Replaced the sky with HDRI / environment imagery
- Preserved and layered foreground powerlines back over the new sky
- Used foreground holdout mattes to keep plate elements sitting correctly over the replacement background
- Repositioned and color-matched inserted background elements to the original plate
- Used projection workflows on geometry driven by the solved camera
- Used roto-based masks to isolate and retain specific foreground / edge elements
- Added volumetric light / flare treatment to help unify the image
- Finished the shot with color balancing, lensing adjustments, defocus, vignette, and regrain
