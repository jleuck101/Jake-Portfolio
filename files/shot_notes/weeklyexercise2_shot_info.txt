Portfolio Summary:
Refined a deliberately broken compositing exercise into a finished character integration shot. Extracted a greenscreen character, repaired alpha and premultiplication issues, regraded the foreground to sit into the environment, and integrated the subject into a CG scene using 2D tracking, projection-based contact/shadow work, light wrap, depth-driven image shaping, and volumetric atmosphere. The script shows multiple targeted fixes, including key adjustments, edge handling, hotspot cleanup, and final focus/atmosphere balancing.

What I Did:
- Keyed and despilled the greenscreen character plate
- Repaired alpha / premultiplication issues in the extraction
- Refined edge quality with erosion, blur, and additional matte cleanup
- Regraded the character to better match the background environment
- 2D tracked the character into the shot
- Used projection-based contact / shadow work to seat the character into the ground plane
- Integrated the character into a CG environment and sky composite
- Used depth-driven shaping and focus tools to help balance the environment
- Added light wrap for stronger foreground/background integration
- Used volumetric light treatment to help unify the scene
- Performed localized cleanup such as hotspot reduction and small roto fixes
- Finished with final image balancing and vignette treatment

Main Challenges:
- Fixing a comp that was intentionally set up with errors rather than building from a clean starting point
- Getting the greenscreen extraction to feel cleaner and more integrated into the environment
- Repairing premult / grade interactions so the foreground did not lift or contaminate the alpha
- Creating believable contact and grounding for the character through projected shadow-style work
- Balancing the foreground subject against a stylized CG / sky environment without breaking the realism too much
