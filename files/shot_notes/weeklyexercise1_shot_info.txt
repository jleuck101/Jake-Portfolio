Portfolio Summary:
Integrated a greenscreen soccer player into a stadium environment while solving several different compositing problems in the same shot. Pulled a chroma key for the foreground player, used a separate luma-based key for the sky replacement, and performed extensive paint work to remove the original stadium name and replace it with custom signage. Refined the foreground matte, controlled spill, cleaned edges, and balanced the final environment so the player, stadium, signage, and sky all felt more cohesive.

What I Did:
- Keyed the greenscreen player plate
- Controlled green spill and refined the foreground extraction
- Cleaned and shaped the alpha with additional matte work
- Integrated the player into the stadium environment
- Pulled a luma-based key to isolate and replace the sky
- Painted out the original stadium name
- Replaced the stadium signage with custom text / graphics
- Performed significant paint cleanup to support the sign replacement and plate repair
- Refined edges and transitions between player, stadium, signage, and sky layers
- Used supporting roto / matte adjustments where needed to improve the final result
- Performed final image balancing to unify the composite

Main Challenges:
- Managing multiple different extraction and repair tasks in the same shot
- Keeping the greenscreen key clean while avoiding harsh edges or contamination
- Using a luma key for the sky replacement without creating weak transitions or broken areas
- Removing and replacing stadium signage cleanly enough that it felt built into the plate
- Making the player sit naturally inside the environment rather than feeling pasted in
