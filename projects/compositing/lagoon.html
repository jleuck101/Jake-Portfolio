The Lagoon

Compositing - Nuke

Overview

This is the big one. By far the largest scale I've worked on on a short film. I was responsible for over a dozen shots, with the work leaning heavily on the face-splitting effect and the challenge of carrying that idea across different environments, different beats, and different pieces of the character's internal conflict.

A lot of the value in this project came from building solutions that could hold up across multiple shots rather than solving only a single moment. That meant developing repeatable setups, adapting them to different lighting conditions and story beats, and keeping the shots readable enough that the technical work still served the scene. The Lagoon taught me a lot about scope, prioritization, and deadlines across a larger body of work. It also gave me more experience tracking and managing multiple shots in Flow (ShotGrid), where the goal was getting the sequence to a strong, consistent level rather than pushing one shot to its absolute peak.

gst020 - Face-Splitting Sequence

This is the main problem-solving shot for the sequence. It was the point where I had to really work out how the split-face idea would function in motion, combining two different takes and getting the alignment, timing, and transitions to hold together cleanly enough that the effect could carry the emotional beat instead of just reading as a trick.

  • Worked out the two-take alignment and split-face blend that became the key technical challenge for the sequence.
  • Built and iterated the face-splitting setup so it could hold up in motion rather than only in a single frame.
  • Timed and adjusted the effect to stay readable through the performance and the edit.
  • Used this shot as the foundation for how the split-character idea could carry across the broader Lagoon work.

echo010 - Humboldt Park Lagoon Pavilion Cleanup

Executed a large-scale 4K paint cleanup on a wide static shot of the Humboldt Park Lagoon pavilion, removing background distractions and rebuilding architectural and natural detail across the frame. This one was less about a flashy effect and more about patient reconstruction, believable patching, and making a wide cleanup hold together without calling attention to itself.

  • Removed equipment tents and other background distractions visible through the pavilion arches.
  • Created clean patches from scratch and rebuilt tree areas from multiple parts of the plate.
  • Painted and patched portions of the pavilion using information sourced elsewhere in the frame.
  • Used manual roto and holdouts for trees, lamps, and overlapping architectural areas.
  • Keyed moving foreground grass where it helped cover and isolate areas during cleanup.
  • Managed slight plate drift while keeping the wider cleanup stable.
  • Used frame holds, time offsets, denoise, and final regrain to keep the cleanup sitting inside the original plate.

lgn010 - Supporting Split-Effect Shot

lgn010 pushes the split-character effect into a fuller body look, giving the shot a broader and more direct read of the technique in motion. It helps show how the Lagoon work expanded visually across the sequence while keeping the effect clear and readable in a wider presentation.
